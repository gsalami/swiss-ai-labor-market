# OECD Employment Outlook 2024

**Institution:** OECD (Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development)  
**Autoren:** OECD  
**Jahr:** 2024  
**URL:** https://www.oecd.org/employment/outlook/

## Abstract
Annual flagship publication on labor markets in OECD countries including Switzerland. Analyzes AI impact on jobs, skills requirements, wages, and policy responses. Special focus on generative AI implications.

## Key Themes 2024

### AI and Employment
- 27% of jobs at high risk of automation in OECD
- Higher for routine cognitive tasks
- New AI tools affecting previously "safe" white-collar jobs
- Complementarity vs. substitution debate ongoing

### Skills Requirements
- Analytical and digital skills increasingly critical
- Social and emotional skills remain important
- Lifelong learning essential
- Current training systems inadequate

### Wage Effects
- AI adoption associated with wage polarization
- High-skill workers benefiting most
- Middle-skill workers facing pressure
- Minimum wage policies as potential buffer

### Policy Recommendations
- Strengthen lifelong learning systems
- Update social protection for changing work patterns
- Support workers in transition
- Ensure inclusive AI benefits
